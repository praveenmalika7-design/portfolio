Successfully completed an undergraduate research project involving the modelling, development, experimental validation, and performance assessment of a hybrid-powered thermoelectric refrigerator. The research aimed to deliver a low-cost, portable, and energy-efficient cooling solution for off-grid and rural applications. A component-level simulation model was developed using MATLAB Simscape and Simulink, validated against prototype measurement data through statistical analysis, and a virtual testbed was built to evaluate and optimize design improvements.

Core Technologies & Tools: MATLAB Simscape MATLAB Simulink Thermoelectric Modules (Peltier) Solar Power System Statistical Validation Thermal Analysis

The Engineering Challenge

Conventional refrigeration systems heavily depend on grid power and rely on refrigerants that are notoriously harmful to the environment. The fundamental challenge was to conceptualise, design, and develop a thermoelectric cooling solution capable of operating effectively on hybrid energy sources (solar and grid). The system had to be tailored for off-grid and rural environments, ensuring robust cooling performance and energy efficiency while minimizing manufacturing costs and completely eliminating the use of hazardous refrigerants.

Research Objectives

  • Develop a low-cost, portable thermoelectric refrigerator designed for off-grid and rural applications.
  • Build an accurate component-level simulation model using MATLAB Simscape and Simulink.
  • Validate the simulation model's accuracy against experimental data extracted from the hardware prototype.
  • Evaluate and benchmark the performance of the hybrid (solar + grid) powered cooling system.
  • Optimise system design parameters using the virtual testbed to substantially reduce prototyping costs.
  • Assess the overall cooling performance, energy efficiency, and thermal management effectiveness of the final system.

My Responsibilities

Research Methodology

  1. Literature Review: Reviewed existing thermoelectric cooling technology, detailed Peltier module characteristics, and modern hybrid energy systems.
  2. System Design: Engineered the thermoelectric refrigerator incorporating a highly versatile hybrid power supply architecture.
  3. Prototype Development: Constructed the functional hardware prototype equipped with accurate thermal instrumentation for detailed measurement.
  4. Experimental Data Collection: Acquired extensive data on temperature, power consumption, and Coefficient of Performance (COP) under both AC and solar power modes.
  5. Simulation Modelling: Formulated a robust component-level model in the MATLAB Simscape and Simulink environment.
  6. Model Validation: Conducted statistical validation to verify simulation outputs against empirically gathered experimental data.
  7. Performance Assessment: Evaluated critical metrics, including COP, total cooling capacity, and power draw across different operational conditions.
  8. Optimization: Leveraged the validated virtual testbed to iterate through design improvements without incurring the cost of rebuilding physical hardware.
  9. Documentation: Prepared and structured the final comprehensive research report and dissertation.
