Fully Automatic Shampoo Filling Machine Design

Company Hemas Consumer Brands (Pvt.) Ltd., Dankotuwa, Sri Lanka
Duration During Internship (November 2025 —“ May 2026)
Contributed to the conceptualization and mechanical design of a Fully Automatic Shampoo Filling Machine intended for future industrial implementation. The project involved developing complete machine structures, filling mechanisms, bottle handling systems, conveyor arrangements, and automated motion systems using SolidWorks. The design incorporated servo-driven positioning systems, bottle gripping mechanisms, conveyor synchronization concepts, safety engineering principles, and automated production line integration.
Technologies Used: SolidWorks Servo Drive Systems Conveyor Design Mechanical Design Industrial Automation Safety Engineering Machine Frame Design Bottle Handling Mechanisms

Engineering Challenge

Developing a fully automated filling system that can handle multiple bottle sizes simultaneously, maintain precise fill volumes, synchronize all subsystems (conveyor, filling heads, capping, labeling interfaces) and comply with food-grade safety and hygiene standards in a high-throughput FMCG environment.

Objectives

  • Design a fully automated shampoo filling machine capable of high-volume industrial production
  • Develop servo-driven positioning and bottle handling systems
  • Design conveyor synchronization and timing mechanisms
  • Incorporate safety engineering and guard design
  • Create comprehensive 3D models and engineering drawings
  • Plan integration with upstream and downstream production line equipment

My Responsibilities

  • Contributed to machine concept development and system architecture design
  • Created detailed 3D models of machine frame, filling station, and bottle handling system using SolidWorks
  • Developed engineering drawings for individual components and assemblies
  • Designed servo-driven motion control concepts for bottle positioning
  • Developed conveyor synchronization and timing concepts
  • Incorporated safety guard design and safety engineering principles
  • Prepared design documentation and engineering reports

Engineering Design Process

  • Requirement Gathering: Defined throughput targets, bottle size range, fill volume accuracy requirements
  • Concept Generation: Developed multiple design concepts for filling station, bottle handling, and conveyor integration
  • Concept Selection: Selected optimal design based on feasibility, cost, and performance criteria
  • 3D Modelling: Built detailed SolidWorks models of all machine subsystems
  • Drawing Package: Created engineering drawings for fabrication reference
  • Design Review: Presented design to engineering team for technical review and feedback
  • Design Optimization: Incorporated review feedback to refine design for future fabrication
